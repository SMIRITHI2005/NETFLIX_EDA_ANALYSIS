 Netflix Movies & TV Shows – Exploratory Data Analysis

Project Overview

This project performs an Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on the Netflix Movies & TV Shows dataset to understand Netflix’s content strategy, growth trends, and distribution across countries, genres, and content types.

The analysis focuses on identifying patterns in content addition over time, differences between Movies and TV Shows, and insights into ratings, duration, and genres.

 Objectives

* Analyze Netflix content growth over the years
* Compare Movies vs TV Shows
* Identify top content-producing countries
* Explore content ratings and genres
* Derive business insights from data

Dataset

Source: Kaggle – Netflix Movies and TV Shows
Records: ~8,800 titles
Columns:

  * show_id
  * type
  * title
  * director
  * cast
  * country
  * date_added
  * release_year
  * rating
  * duration
  * listed_in
  * description

Tools & Libraries

* Python
* Pandas
* NumPy
* Matplotlib
* Seaborn
* Jupyter Notebook


Workflow

1. Data Loading & Inspection
2. Data Cleaning & Missing Value Handling
3. Feature Engineering (date, duration, country, genre)
4. Univariate, Bivariate & Multivariate Analysis
5. Data Visualization
6. Business Insights & Recommendations

Key Visualizations

* Content added over years (Line chart)
* Movies vs TV Shows distribution (Bar chart)
* Top 10 countries by content count
* Ratings distribution
* Genre popularity
* Movie duration & TV show seasons analysis

 Key Insights

* Netflix content grew rapidly after 2016
* Movies make up ~70% of the catalog
* The United States dominates content production
* TV Shows are mostly limited to 1 season
* Drama and International content are the most common genres
* Mature-rated content (TV-MA) is highly prevalent



Conclusion

The analysis reveals Netflix’s strategic shift toward rapid content expansion, internationalization, and a strong focus on movies and mature-rated content.
